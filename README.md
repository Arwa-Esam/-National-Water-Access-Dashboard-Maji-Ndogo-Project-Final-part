## üîç Key Insights from the Public Water Dashboard

### ‚úÖ 1. National Water Access Is Still Low
Only **47.8%** of the population currently has access to basic water services.  
This means **over half of Maji Ndogo** still struggles with safe, consistent water access, highlighting a major need for continued investment.

---

### ‚úÖ 2. 4 Million People Recently Gained Water Access
The project has already enabled **4 million individuals** to obtain basic water access.  
This confirms that the interventions are having a meaningful and measurable impact.

---

### ‚úÖ 3. Budget Spending Is Slightly Above Target
Total spending has reached **$33.8M**, which is **10.69% higher** than the planned budget.  
This signals potential **cost overruns** and the need for more careful monitoring as the project continues.

---

### ‚úÖ 4. Overall Project Completion Is Still Low
The project is currently **22% complete**, despite significant spending.  
This mismatch between **spending vs. completion** suggests:
- Work is progressing slowly
- Some areas might require operational adjustments
- Potential bottlenecks in field execution

---

### ‚úÖ 5. Most Money Is Concentrated in 2 Provinces
A large portion of the budget is going to:
- **Sokoto (~$11.1M)**
- **Kilimani (~$6.66M)**

These provinces are consuming the majority of funds, likely due to:
- High infrastructure needs
- Larger population sizes
- More improvement activities

---

### ‚úÖ 6. RO Filter Installations Are the Highest-Cost Activity
The single most expensive improvement category is:
- **Install RO Filter ‚Äì $14M**

This indicates:
- High demand for clean water treatment
- RO filters being the preferred method for long-term water safety
- A need to assess if this cost level is sustainable

---

### ‚úÖ 7. Public Taps and Drilling Wells Are Also Major Cost Drivers
Additional spending highlights:
- **Public Taps: $9M**
- **Drill Wells: $8M**

These improvements directly support community access in rural and low-infrastructure areas.

---

### ‚úÖ 8. Some Provinces Have Low Spending but High Needs
Provinces like **Amanzi** and **Hawassa** have noticeably lower costs despite population needs.  
This may indicate:
- Fewer improvement activities
- Delays in contractor execution
- Lower infrastructure capacity  
These areas may require further field review.

---

### ‚úÖ 9. Total Improvements Completed: 25K
A total of **25,000 improvements** have been completed so far, showing strong operational activity despite budget challenges.

---

### üß† Summary
